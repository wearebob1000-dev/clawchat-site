About the Property

17 N 5th Avenue is a legal two-family home in North Long Branch, approximately one mile from the beach. This report covers the lower unit only. The lower unit features 3 bedrooms and 1 bathroom on the main floor, an open living room, dine-in kitchen, plus a finished basement with a second bathroom — effectively a 3 BD / 2 BA layout with bonus living space.

The property previously rented at $3,100/month combined. The finished basement adds meaningful appeal — usable as a 4th sleeping area, game room, or den for summer guests, which directly supports premium seasonal pricing.

Strategy Comparison

Pros & Cons

OPTION A — Seasonal

✅ Pros

  • ~$6K more net annually vs Option B
  • 1 mile to beach — strong NJ Shore demand
  • Finished basement = bonus sleeping area
  • Fire pit / outdoor space supports premium pricing
  • Monmouth University off-season tenant pool

⚠️ Cons

  • $5–12K IKEA furnishing cost upfront
  • Higher mgmt burden (cleaning, turnover)
  • Vacancy risk if not listed early
  • More wear from short-term guests
  • Long Branch STR permit required — verify
OPTION B — Annual

✅ Pros

  • Stable, predictable monthly income
  • Zero furnishing cost
  • Low management overhead
  • No STR permit concerns
  • Lower reno budget (~$33K vs ~$41K)

⚠️ Cons

  • ~$6K less net per year vs seasonal
  • Locked in even if rental market rises
  • Tenant issues = longer resolution
Landscaping Note

🌲 Back Fence Privacy Screening

Recommended: Two-layer approach for faster, denser results:

Layer 1 (Fast screen, 1–2 seasons): Emerald Green Arborvitae every 3 feet along back fence. Grows 6–9 inches/year, dense year-round, cold-hardy. ~$50–100/tree installed.

Layer 2 (Optional bonus): Knockout Roses or ornamental grasses in front of evergreens — adds color from the yard side and makes the space feel more like a resort garden than a backyard fence line. Directly supports summer rental appeal and pricing.

📋 Recommendation

Both options pay back the renovation in roughly 12 months — so this is less a financial decision and more an operational preference. Seasonal earns ~$6K more per year but requires management (listings, cleaning, turnover). Annual is set-it-and-forget-it at a nearly identical payback timeline, with lower upfront cost since there's no furnishing.

If you have the bandwidth or a local property manager, seasonal is the better play — the finished basement and outdoor entertaining space give this unit a real competitive edge for groups willing to pay $10–12K for the summer. List it now and you can catch summer 2026.

Key next steps: (1) Verify Long Branch STR permit requirements before committing to seasonal. (2) Get 2–3 contractor quotes immediately — target completion by late May. (3) If seasonal, list on Airbnb, VRBO, and Shore House NJ ASAP — summer books early. (4) IKEA Edison is 45 min away for an efficient furnishing run.
